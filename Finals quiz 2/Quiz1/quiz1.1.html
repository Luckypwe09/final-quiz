My Favorite Place

Palawan

Palawan is a province of the Philippines located in the western part of the country between the South China Sea (West Philippine Sea) and the Sulu Sea, and it is often called the “Last Ecological Frontier” because of its rich biodiversity and well-preserved natural environment. Its capital is Puerto Princesa City, and the province is composed of the main Palawan Island along with other notable islands such as Coron, Busuanga, and Culion. Palawan features mountainous landscapes, limestone cliffs, white-sand beaches, coral reefs, mangrove forests, and unique natural attractions, including the Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Palawan’s history began with early indigenous settlements and trade with nearby Asian regions before Spanish colonization in the 16th century, when it was called Paragua and brought under Spanish rule. During World War II, it was the site of the 1944 Palawan Massacre involving American prisoners of war. After the war, Palawan became part of the independent Philippines in 1946, and in recent years it has focused on conservation and sustainable development due to its rich natural resources.

Palawan is my favorite because of its natural beauty, peaceful environment, and rich culture. The province is known for its clear waters, white-sand beaches, limestone cliffs, and diverse marine life, which make it both relaxing and exciting to explore. Its commitment to protecting nature and preserving indigenous culture also makes Palawan special, as it shows the importance of caring for the environment while enjoying its beauty.
